Case Study: Bulgaria

Workflow

Mapping air quality, land cover transitions, and population exposure.

The project followed a GIS-based workflow to analyze air quality, land cover change, and population exposure in Bulgaria. CAMS pollutant data for NO2, PM2.5, and PM10 were downloaded, clipped to the Bulgarian national boundary, and processed into December 2023 pollutant maps and annual average maps for 2021 and 2023.

The 2023 annual pollutant maps for Bulgaria were reclassified into concentration classes according to air-quality thresholds, with pollutant concentrations expressed in µg/m³. Annual mean change maps were then created by subtracting the 2021 pollutant averages from the 2023 averages, allowing spatial increases and decreases in pollution across Bulgaria to be identified.

Land cover data from ESRI 10 m Annual Land Cover for 2021 and 2023 were processed to create a Bulgaria land cover transition map. The transition analysis was used to calculate stable pixels, gains, and losses for the assigned land cover class, supporting interpretation of how land cover dynamics may relate to environmental change.

Finally, population data and administrative boundaries for Bulgaria were combined with pollutant layers through zonal statistics. Bivariate maps and chart layers were produced to compare population distribution with pollutant concentration levels. The processed layers, statistical tables, charts, legends, and maps were integrated into a website and WebGIS for clear and interactive presentation.

StepProcessOutput / Purpose
1Data CollectionCAMS pollutant data, ESRI 10 m land cover, WorldPop population data, and administrative boundaries for Bulgaria.
2Pre-processingClip layers to the Bulgarian boundary, organize raster data, and prepare QGIS layers.
3Pollutant MappingCreate December 2023 pollutant maps and annual average maps for NO2, PM2.5, and PM10 in Bulgaria.
4Annual Change DetectionCalculate 2023 annual average minus 2021 annual average to detect pollution increase and decrease.
5Land Cover ChangeGenerate the 2021-2023 Bulgaria land cover transition raster and classify stable, gain, and loss pixels.
6Zonal StatisticsCompute regional pollutant and population statistics using Bulgarian administrative boundaries.
7Population Exposure AnalysisCreate bivariate layers combining pollutant classes with population classes.
8Website/WebGISPublish maps, tables, charts, legends, and explanations in the website and WebGIS.
9Final SubmissionSubmit processed GIS outputs and website/WebGIS code through active download links.
