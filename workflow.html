Case Study: Bulgaria

Workflow

Mapping air quality, land cover transitions, and population exposure.

The project followed a GIS-based workflow to analyse air quality, land cover change, and population exposure in Bulgaria. Multiple environmental and socio-economic datasets were integrated to investigate the relationships between pollution, land cover dynamics, and population distribution.

CAMS atmospheric data for NO2, PM2.5, and PM10 were downloaded and clipped to the Bulgarian national boundary. Monthly data were processed to generate December 2023 pollutant maps and annual average concentration maps for 2021 and 2023. The 2023 annual average maps were subsequently reclassified into concentration classes according to predefined air-quality thresholds, producing Concentration Classification maps with pollutant concentrations expressed in µg/m3. Finally, Annual Mean Absolute Change (AMAC) maps were created by subtracting the 2021 annual averages from the 2023 annual averages, allowing areas with increasing or decreasing pollution levels to be identified.

Land cover analysis was performed using the ESRI 10 m Annual Land Cover datasets for 2021 and 2023. Three land cover categories were analysed: Built-up Areas, Crops, and Trees, associated respectively with NO2, PM2.5, and PM10. Land Cover Change (LCC) maps were generated to identify Stable, Gain, and Loss pixels between 2021 and 2023. The resulting transition layers were further analysed using zonal statistics to summarize regional land cover dynamics. Statistical charts describing the mean, minimum, and maximum values of Stable, Gain, and Loss categories were produced together with summary tables reporting the proportion of Stable pixels and the dominant transition pathways. Pixel percentages were calculated using the original raster geometry in EPSG:4326, while area calculations were performed in the equal-area projection EPSG:3035 to ensure accurate area estimation.

Population exposure analysis was carried out by combining WorldPop population data, Bulgarian administrative boundaries, and pollutant concentration layers. Zonal statistics were used to aggregate population and pollution information at the administrative-unit level. Pollutant concentration classes were then combined with population classes to generate Bivariate Maps, highlighting areas where elevated pollution levels coincide with high population density. Additional Population Exposure Charts and summary tables were created to support quantitative interpretation of exposure patterns across Bulgaria.

All processed datasets, maps, statistical outputs, charts, legends, and explanatory content were integrated into a website and WebGIS platform. The final system enables interactive visualization of pollution patterns, land cover dynamics, and population exposure, providing a comprehensive overview of environmental conditions in Bulgaria.

Workflow Diagram

StepProcessOutput / Purpose
1Data CollectionAcquire CAMS pollutant data, ESRI 10 m Annual Land Cover data, WorldPop population data, and Bulgarian administrative boundaries.
2Data Pre-processingClip datasets to the Bulgarian national boundary, organize raster layers, and prepare GIS datasets for analysis.
3December 2023 Pollution MappingGenerate December 2023 concentration maps for NO2, PM2.5, and PM10.
4Annual Average Pollution MappingCalculate 2021 and 2023 annual average pollutant concentration maps.
5Concentration ClassificationReclassify 2023 annual averages into concentration classes according to air-quality thresholds.
6Annual Mean Absolute Change (AMAC)Calculate 2023 annual average minus 2021 annual average to identify pollution increase and decrease.
7Land Cover Change DetectionGenerate 2021–2023 Land Cover Change maps for Built-up Areas, Crops, and Trees and classify Stable, Gain, and Loss pixels.
8Land Cover StatisticsProduce zonal statistics layers, Stable/Gain/Loss charts, and land cover transition statistical tables.
9Population Exposure AnalysisGenerate bivariate maps, exposure charts, and population exposure tables by combining pollution and population data.
10Website and WebGIS DevelopmentIntegrate maps, tables, charts, legends, and explanatory text into the website and WebGIS platform.
11Final SubmissionPublish the website and WebGIS and provide active download links for project deliverables.
